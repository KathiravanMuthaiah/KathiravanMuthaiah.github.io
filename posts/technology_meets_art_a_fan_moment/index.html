Okie now let us enjoy the moment. There are certain songs that stay with you—not just as music, but as emotions you can revisit anytime. Recently, I spent some quiet time with one of my favourite Tamil songs: Watch on YouTube.

This song has always fascinated me with its beautiful lyrics and mesmerising music. As a fan of art and technology, I wanted to explore how far today’s tools can help me experience a song in new dimensions—without ever taking away from the originality or essence of the work.

To test the artistic ability of AI

I began by taking the original Tamil lyrics and, with the help of ChatGPT, created an English version that retained the emotional essence of the song. It wasn’t a literal translation—it was more like a heartfelt interpretation. After a few rounds of revisions, I had a version that I felt captured the mood I experience whenever I listen to the song.

This process itself was enjoyable. It was a dialogue between my heart, the song’s essence, and a tool that could reflect and refine my thoughts. It is always fun to talk to a reflection of you with more abilities to express easily what you felt.

Once you get the output as a developer you always wanted to share and see the output. A moment of visualising the output always a sense of satisfaction. I also wanted to visualise the output in an art form.

Started imagining how the scene would be

I turned them into prompts to generate images using ChatGPT and the Flux model for image generation. Watching the tool produce visual interpretations of the emotion I felt was a moment of pure euphoria.

Prompt used to generate image in both the AI tools:

A somber, emotional scene set in a quiet, desolate landscape at dusk. A lone figure, dressed in dark, tattered clothing, sits on the ground, head bowed, surrounded by broken, feathered wings scattered like remnants of shattered dreams. The background is vast and barren, with a faint, blurred horizon symbolizing lost direction. Fading silhouettes of memories, like translucent shadows, drift into the air. 

Nearby, a small pool of water reflects the figure's tear-streaked face, with their eyes resembling a fish swimming in sorrow. In the distance, a faintly visible figure, illuminated by a soft golden glow, stands laughing, creating a stark emotional contrast. The overall atmosphere is melancholic, with muted earthy tones, soft lighting, and a gentle breeze stirring the scene.

I got two beatifull images which reflect the ability of the tool and also how fascinating to see an art form as a complete output of a machine.

Let us add music to the words and scene

Finally, I took my English lyrics to suno.ai to generate a tune and transform it into a full-fledged song. Hearing my words come to life with a melody was a surreal experience.

While I know this is not the original composition and cannot match the mastery of the creators, it gave me a profound sense of satisfaction. It felt like technology allowed me to participate in the art—not as a creator replacing the original, but as a fan enjoying a customised journey through the music.

I just felt like the exact moment of Robinson Crusoe [Cast Away]: ‘Yeah!!! Look what I have created. I HAVE MADE FIRE, I, HAVE MADE FIRE’ :) :) :)

I created this image without thinking that this is apt for this blog. You will always use your learning somewhere. My teacher used to say that. When getting old, I started to believe it :) .

Yes, it was a moment of achievement and a sense of satisfaction that it is unique, new and a lovable result.

This journey wasn’t about recreating art. It was about celebrating art. Technology, in this case, became a bridge between my admiration for the original work and my curiosity to interact with it in a new way.

As a technologist, I realised that these tools, image generation models, and AI music platforms allow us to experience creativity at an abstract level—to visualise, to reinterpret, and to emotionally engage in ways that were once impossible.

And as a fan, the joy lies in that small sense of personal connection: a moment of happiness where art and technology meet in harmony.

Again and again and again saying AI tools will never replace the soul of human creativity. But they can help us, as admirers of art, explore tiny reflections of that creativity in our own way—creating moments of personal joy that honor, not replace, the original masterpiece.
